# BENCHMARK.md — Market Research Summary

## Purpose
This file summarizes the benchmark research used to shape the product.

---

## 1. Product categories observed
The market spans several overlapping categories:
- professional network + recruiting platform
- job board
- ATS
- recruiting CRM
- ats

Your product should start as:
> a multi-tenant recruiting platform with candidate profiles, job discovery, and ATS-lite workflow

---

## 2. Platforms studied
### LinkedIn Jobs / Talent Solutions
Strong in:
- professional identity
- recruiter collaboration
- sourcing
- candidate discovery
- employer brand presence

### Indeed
Strong in:
- high-volume discovery
- job distribution
- applicant screening
- employer dashboarding
- low-friction search/apply flows

### Glassdoor
Strong in:
- employer brand context
- salaries
- candidate trust signals
- company decision support

### ZipRecruiter
Strong in:
- candidate-job matching
- speed of distribution
- recruiter efficiency

### Wellfound
Strong in:
- startup hiring niche
- transparent role context
- candidate-company fit for tech/startup jobs

### Simplify
Strong in:
- application speed
- autofill/reuse
- candidate-side productivity

### Gloat / asi_do style products
Strong in:
- skills-based opportunity matching
- marketplace logic
- future-facing internal/external mobility concepts

---

## 3. Common patterns across winners
The strongest platforms converge around four product layers:
1. discovery of opportunities
2. reusable candidate identity
3. structured employer workspace
4. applicant pipeline management

---

## 4. Most important benchmark insights
1. **Reduce candidate friction aggressively**
   Reusable profiles and quick apply matter.

2. **Employer workflow must be structured**
   Job posting alone is not enough; pipeline tooling matters.

3. **Permissions matter earlier than teams expect**
   Once companies add multiple recruiters/managers, RBAC becomes essential.

4. **Mobile experience is strategic**
   Candidates especially operate heavily from phones.

5. **Trust signals matter**
   Company credibility, moderation, and clarity improve conversions.

6. **The future points toward matching**
   Skills, recommendations, and automation become natural expansion paths after MVP.

---

## 5. Product implications for this project
### Must-have at MVP
- candidate profile + CV
- public jobs
- search/filter
- quick apply
- employer workspace
- ATS-lite stages
- tenant roles and permissions
- notifications
- mobile-first installable app

### Later expansion
- semantic/skills matching
- AI assistance
- interview scheduling
- talent pools / CRM
- employer branding enhancements
- analytics / exports
- premium visibility products

---

## 6. Strategic recommendation
Do **not** begin by cloning the entire social graph/networking surface of LinkedIn.

Begin with:
- job marketplace
- candidate profile
- employer workspace
- ATS-lite
- RBAC
- mobile-first PWA

Then expand once the hiring loop works end to end.
